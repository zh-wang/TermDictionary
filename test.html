attend

at・tend /əténd/

【動詞】 【他動詞】

1a〈会などに〉出席する; 〈儀式に〉参列する.
用例
  • attend a meeting [funeral] 会合に出席[葬儀に参列]する.

b〈学校・教会に〉通う 《★【比較】 go to を用いるほうが一般的》.
用例
  • He attends school regularly. 彼はいつも休まずに学校へ行く.

2a〈召し使いなどが〉〈人の〉世話をする，〈人に〉随行する.
用例
  • The bridesmaid attends the bride. ブライドメードは花嫁の世話をします.

b〈医師・看護婦などが〉〈患者に〉付き添う，〈患者の〉看護[治療]をする.
用例
  • She attended her sick mother. 彼女は病気の母の面倒をみた.
  • The patient was attended by a doctor. 病人には医者がついていた.

開く


3(結果として)〈…に〉伴う 《★しばしば受身で用い，前置詞は with，by》.
用例
  • Success often attends hard work. 勤勉にはよく成功が伴う.
  • The enterprise was attended with much difficulty. その事業には多大の困難が伴った.

開く


【自動詞】

1a出席する，参列する.
用例
  • He attends regularly. 彼は決まって出席する.

b〔+at+(代)名詞〕《主に英国で用いられる》〔…に〕出席する.
2a注意する，注意して聞く.
用例
  • Please attend carefully. よく注意して聞いてください.

b〔+to+(代)名詞〕〔人の言葉などに〕注意する，注意して聞く.
用例
  • Attend to your teacher [what your teacher says]. 先生の言われることをよく聞いていなさい.

3〔+to+(代)名詞〕〔仕事などに〕精を出す，専心する.
用例
  • attend to business 商売に精を出す.

4〔+前置詞+(代)名詞〕《★受身可》
a〔人などの〕世話をする，面倒を見る，ご用を聞く 〔to〕.
用例
  • Who attends to the baby when you're at work? あなたが仕事をしている時だれが赤ん坊の世話をするのか.
  • Are you being attended to, ma'am? [店員が婦人客に] だれかご用を伺っていますでしょうか.

開く


b〈医者・看護婦などが〉〔病人・傷などの〕世話をする，手当てをする，看護する 〔to，on，upon〕.
用例
  • Three doctors attended on the patient. 3人の医者が患者に付き添った.

c〈召使などが〉〔人に〕(従者として)仕える，付き添う 〔on，upon〕.
用例
  • She had three servants attending on [upon] her. 彼女は 3 人の召使にかしずかれていた.

d〔…に〕伴う 〔on，upon〕.
用例
  • Many difficulties attended on [upon] (the) development of the product. その製品の開発には多くの困難が伴った.

【語源】

ラテン語「心を向ける，注意する」の意 (AT‐+tendere 「伸ばす，向ける」); 【名詞】 attendance，attention

attend

【動詞】

1. （に）注意を与える(give heed (to))
用例
  • The children in the audience attended the recital quietly 客席の子供は静かにリサイタルを聴いていたThey attended to everything he said 彼らは彼が言ったことをすべて傾聴した

2. のために働くか、使用人である(work for or be a servant to)
用例
  • She attends the old lady in the wheelchair 彼女は、車椅子の老婦人に付き添う

3. 取り仕切る、あるいは扱う(take charge of or deal with)
用例
  • I must attend to this matter 私はこの問題に注意しなければならない

4. （ミーティング、礼拝、大学）等に出席する(be present at (meetings, church services, university) , etc.)
用例
  • She attends class regularly 彼女は定期的に授業に出るI rarely attend services at my church 私はめったに教会の礼拝に参加しない

5. 状況として付随する、その結果あとに続く(to accompany as a circumstance or follow as a result)
用例
  • Menuhin's playing was attended by a 15-minute standing ovation メニューインの演奏のあと15分のスタンディング・オベーションがあった

Wiktionary英語版

出典：Wiktionary

attend

出典:『Wiktionary』 (2013/12/21 22:28 UTC 版)

発音

  • 韻: -ɛnd

語源 1

From Middle English attenden, atenden, from 古期英語 ātendan (“to set on fire, kindle, inflame, trouble, perplex”), equivalent to a- +‎ tend.

動詞

attend (三人称単数 現在形 attends, 現在分詞 attending, 過去形および過去分詞形 attended)

  1. Alternative form of atend ("to kindle").
関連する語
  • tend
  • tinder

語源 2

From Middle English attenden, atenden, from Old French atendre (“to attend, listen”), from Latin attendere (“to stretch toward, give heed to”), from ad (“to”) + tendere (“to stretch”); see tend and compare attempt.

動詞

attend (三人称単数 現在形 attends, 現在分詞 attending, 過去形および過去分詞形 attended)

  1. (archaic, transitive) To listen to (something または someone); to pay attention to; regard; heed. [from 15th c.]
    • Sir Philip Sidney (1554-1586)
      The diligent pilot in a dangerous tempest doth not attend the unskilful words of the passenger.
  2. (archaic, intransitive) To listen (to, unto). [from 15th c.]
  3. ​(intransitive) To turn one's consideration (to); to deal with (a task, problem, concern etc.), to look after. [from 15th c.]
    Secretaries attend to correspondence.
    • 1918, W. B. Maxwell, chapter 15, The Mirror and the Lamp:
      Edward Churchill still attended to his work in a hopeless mechanical manner like a sleep-walker who walks safely on a well-known round. But his Roman collar galled him, his cossack stifled him, his biretta was as uncomfortable as a merry-andrew's cap and bells.
  4. (transitive) To wait upon as a servant etc.; to accompany to assist (someone). [from 15th c.]
    Valets attend to their employer's wardrobe.
    • Edmund Spenser (c.1552–1599)
      The fifth had charge sick persons to attend.
    • William Shakespeare (1564-1616)
      Attends the emperor in his royal court.
    • Thomas Macaulay (1800-1859)
      With a sore heart and a gloomy brow, he prepared to attend William thither.
  5. ​(transitive) To be present at (an event または place) in order to take part in some action or proceedings. [from 17th c.]
    Children must attend primary school.
    • 1898, Winston Churchill, chapter 5, The Celebrity:
      In the eyes of Mr. Farquhar Fenelon Cooke the apotheosis of the Celebrity was complete. The people of Asquith were not only willing to attend the house-warming, but had been worked up to the pitch of eagerness. The Celebrity as a matter of course was master of ceremonies.
    • 1994, Nelson Mandela, Long Walk to Freedom, Abacus 2010, p. 20:
      I attended a one-room school next door to the palace and studied English, Xhosa, history and geography.
  6. To be present with; to accompany; to be united or consequent to.
    a measure attended with ill effects
    • John Dryden (1631-1700)
      What cares must then attend the toiling swain.
    • 1922, Ben Travers, chapter 5, A Cuckoo in the Nest:
      The most rapid and most seductive transition in all human nature is that which attends the palliation of a ravenous appetite. There is something humiliating about it. […] Can those harmless but refined fellow-diners be the selfish cads whose gluttony and personal appearance so raised your contemptuous wrath on your arrival?
  7. To wait for; to await; to remain, abide, or be in store for.
    • John Locke (1632-1705)
      the state that attends all men after this
    • John Dryden (1631-1700)
      Three days I promised to attend my doom.
同意語
  • (listen to): behear
