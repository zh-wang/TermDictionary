quick

quick /kwík/

形容詞

(quick・er，quick・est; more quick，most quick)
1a急速な，迅速な; すばやい，敏捷(びんしよう)な (⇔slow) 《★【類語】 quick は行動がすばやい・時間が短い; rapid，fast は共に動作・行動などが速いの意であるが，rapid は動きそのものを，fast は動いている人・物を重視する; swift は運動が滑らかな・軽い; speedy は速度が速い・行動がすばやい》.
a quick reader 読むのが速い人, 速読家.
in quick motion 迅速に.
quick on one's feet 足が速い.
walk at a quick pace 速い足どりで歩く.
Be quick (about it)! (それを)早く(しろ)!
Let's have a quick jog before breakfast. 朝食前にひと走りしてこよう.
b叙述的用法の形容詞 〔+to do〕〈…するのが〉早くて.
She's quick to understand. 彼女は理解が早い.
He's quick to take offense. 彼はすぐにかっとなる.
c叙述的用法の形容詞 〔+前+(代)名〕〔…に〕早くて 〔at，in〕; 〔…が〕早くて 〔of〕.
She's quick at figures [at learning languages]. 彼女は計算が[外国語を覚えるのが]早い.
He's quick in his decisions [in answering questions]. 彼は決断が速い[よくてきぱきと質問に答える].
He's quick of hearing [understanding]. 彼は耳が[のみこみが]早い.
2a理解の早い，さとい; 利口な.
She has a quick mind. 彼女は頭の回転が速い.
That boy is not very quick. あの子はあまり頭がよくない.
b敏感な，鋭い.
  • She has a quick ear. 彼女は敏感な耳をもっている.

3せっかちな，怒りっぽい.
  • a quick temper 短気 (cf. quick‐tempered).

4〈曲がり方など〉急な.
  • make a quick turn 急に進路を変える.

5(比較なし) 《古語》
a生きている.
b[the quick; 名詞的に; 複数扱い] 生きている人々.
  • the quick and the dead 生者と死者.

Eゲイト英和辞典

quick

音節quick発音記号kwɪ́k変化～･er; ～･est
コア（動作・理解・反応などが）すばやい
速い▷形容詞1
短時間の▷形容詞2
理解がはやい▷形容詞3
短気な▷形容詞4

形容詞
1（動作・行動などが）速い，すばやい，機敏な（←→slow）⇒『fast~１』【ネットワーク】
  • a quick worker
    仕事の速い人
  • We walked at a very quick pace.
    私たちは足早に歩いた
  • He is quick with his fists.
    彼は手が早い［すぐに暴力をふるう］
  • This is an urgent matter, so please be quick about it.
    これは緊急を要する件なので，（それに関しては）急いでください
  • You are quick to find fault with others.
    君はすぐに他人のあら捜しをするね

2（行動・出来事などが）短時間の，一瞬の
  • Can I take a quick shower before we go out?
    出かけるまえに，さっとシャワーを浴びてもいい?
  • We had to make a quick decision about it.
    私たちはすぐにそれについて決断しなければならなかった
  • She gave me a quick glance.
    彼女は私をちらっと見た

3（人が）〈…の〉理解がはやい，〈…について〉頭の回転の速い〈at/in/with〉
  • a quick learner
    物覚えのはやい人
  • He has a quick mind.
    彼は理解が早い
  • She is very quick at figures.
    彼女は計算がとても速い

4((限定))（人・性格が）短気な，気の短い；（感覚が）敏感な，鋭い
  • You have a quick temper.
    君は短気だね
  • He has a quick ear for music.
    彼は音楽に対する鋭い耳を持っている

5（カーブなどが）急な，鋭い
6((古))（人・動植物が）生きている；（火が）勢いよく燃える
  • a quick hedge
    生け垣
  • a quick flame
    烈火

成句a quick one [half, pint]
((おもに英口))（ぐいっと飲む酒の）一杯
  • Have you got time for a quick one before we go home?
    家に帰る前にきゅっと一杯やる時間はありますか

副詞
1速く，急いで，すばやく
  • the way to get rich quick
    すぐ金持ちになる方法
  • Come as quick as you can!
    できるだけ急いで来なさい

2((複合語を作って))速く…（分詞の前につけて用いる）
  • quick‐growing plants
    成長の早い植物

名詞
((ふつうthe ～))1（つめの下・傷口などの）生身の部分，皮膚の敏感な所
2((the ～))【集合】((古))生きている人々
  • the quick and the dead
    生ける者と死せし者

成句cut |a| |person| to the quick
（ことばなどが）（人）の心を深く傷つける
  • My careless remarks cut her to the quick.
    私の不注意なことばが彼女をひどく傷つけた

語法
①quicklyよりも口語的で，語調が強く，動作を表す動詞とともに多く用いられる
②感嘆文以外では通例動詞の後に置かれる

quick

出典:『Wiktionary』 (2016/05/19 23:59 UTC 版)

発音

  • IPA(key): /kwɪk/
  • 韻: -ɪk

語源

PIE root
*gʷeyh₃-

From Middle English quik or quic, from 古期英語 cwic ‎(“alive”), from Proto-Germanic *kwikwaz, from Proto-Indo-European *gʷih₃wós ‎(“alive”), from *gʷeyh₃- ‎(“to live”), *gʷeih₃w- ‎(“to live”). Cognate with Dutch kwik and kwiek, German keck, Swedish kvick; and (from Indo-European) with Ancient Greek βίος ‎(bíos, “life”), Latin vivus, Lithuanian gývas ‎(“alive”), Latvian dzīvs ‎(“alive”), Russian живо́й ‎(živój), Welsh byw ‎(“alive”), Irish beo ‎(“alive”), biathaim ‎(“nourish”), Kurdish jîn ‎(“to live”) and jiyan ‎(“life”), giyan ‎(“soul”), can ‎(“soul”), Sanskrit जीव ‎(jīva, “living”).

形容詞

quick ‎(comparative quicker, superlative quickest)

  1. Moving with speed, rapidity or swiftness, or capable of doing so; rapid; fast.
    I ran to the station – but I wasn't quick enough.
    He's a quick runner.
  2. Occurring in a short time; happening or done rapidly.
    That was a quick meal.
  3. Lively, fast-thinking, witty, intelligent.
    You have to be very quick to be able to compete in ad-lib theatrics.
  4. Mentally agile, alert, perceptive.
    My father is old but he still has a quick wit.
  5. Of temper: easily aroused to anger; quick-tempered.
    • Latimer
      The bishop was somewhat quick with them, and signified that he was much offended.
  6. (archaic) Alive, living.
    • Bible, 2 Timothy iv. 1
      the Lord Jesus Christ, who shall judge the quick and the dead
    • Herbert
      Man is no star, but a quick coal / Of mortal fire.
    • 1874, James Thomson, The City of Dreadful Night, X
      The inmost oratory of my soul,
      Wherein thou ever dwellest quick or dead,
      Is black with grief eternal for thy sake.
  7. (archaic) Pregnant, especially at the stage where the foetus's movements can be felt; figuratively, alive with some emotion or feeling.
    • Shakespeare
      she's quick; the child brags in her belly already: tis yours
  8. Of water: flowing.
  9. Burning, flammable, fiery.
  10. Fresh; bracing; sharp; keen.
    • Shakespeare
      The air is quick there, / And it pierces and sharpens the stomach.
  11. (mining, of a vein of ore) productive; not "dead" or barren

同意語

  • (moving with speed): fast, speedy, rapid, swift

反意語

  • (moving with speed): slow

  1. quickly

派生語

名詞

quick ‎(複数形 quicks)

  1. raw or sensitive flesh, especially that underneath finger and toe nails.
  2. plants used in making a quickset hedge
    • Evelyn
      The works […] are curiously hedged with quick.
  3. The life; the mortal point; a vital part; a part susceptible to serious injury or keen feeling.
    • Latimer
      This test nippeth, […] this toucheth the quick.
    • Fuller
      How feebly and unlike themselves they reason when they come to the quick of the difference!
  4. quitchgrass
    (Can we find かつ add a quotation of Tennyson to this entry?)
  5. (cricket) A fast bowler.

派生語

  • to the quick

動詞

quick ‎(三人称単数 現在形 quicks, 現在分詞 quicking, 過去形および過去分詞形 quicked)

  1. (transitive) To amalgamate surfaces prior to gilding or silvering by dipping them into a solution of mercury in nitric acid.
  2. (transitive, archaic, poetic) To quicken.
    • Thomas Hardy
      I rose as if quicked by a spur I was bound to obey.
